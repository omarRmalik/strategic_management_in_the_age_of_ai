# Part II

# Course Design

> *"Education should not simply transfer knowledge; it should develop the capacity to generate new knowledge. This course is designed to prepare students not only to understand strategic management, but to participate in its evolution."*

---

# Course Description

## Catalog Description

Strategic Management in the Age of Artificial Intelligence examines how artificial intelligence is transforming competitive strategy, organizational capabilities, executive decision making, innovation, and enterprise performance. The course integrates classical strategic management theories—including industry analysis, the Resource-Based View, Dynamic Capabilities, Organizational Learning, Transaction Cost Economics, and Strategic Leadership—with contemporary scholarship on artificial intelligence, digital transformation, platform ecosystems, and human–AI collaboration.

Students critically evaluate how AI influences the assumptions underlying strategic management while developing practical competencies through case analyses, AI laboratories, executive briefings, and a semester-long consulting engagement. Emphasis is placed on evidence-based decision making, responsible AI governance, organizational transformation, and strategic leadership in increasingly AI-enabled organizations.

The course combines foundational academic literature, contemporary research, Harvard Business School cases, executive reports, and applied consulting methodologies to prepare students for leadership roles in organizations navigating technological disruption.

---

# Prerequisites

Students should have completed an introductory course in strategic management or possess equivalent knowledge of organizational behavior, management, marketing, finance, or economics.

No programming experience is required.

Students are expected to possess graduate-level reading, writing, and analytical skills and be prepared to engage critically with scholarly literature.

---

# Course Objectives

The primary objective of this course is to develop strategic leaders capable of integrating classical strategic management theory with contemporary artificial intelligence research to formulate, evaluate, and implement organizational strategy.

By the conclusion of the course students should understand:

* why strategic management theories emerged,
* the assumptions underlying those theories,
* how artificial intelligence changes those assumptions,
* how organizations create competitive advantage using AI,
* how executives should govern AI responsibly,
* and how strategic management itself may evolve over the coming decades.

---

# Educational Goals

The course seeks to develop five complementary dimensions of executive capability.

## 1. Strategic Thinking

Students learn to diagnose complex organizational problems, evaluate competitive environments, identify strategic opportunities, and formulate evidence-based recommendations.

---

## 2. Scholarly Reasoning

Students engage directly with seminal and contemporary research, compare competing theoretical perspectives, and critically evaluate emerging scholarship.

---

## 3. Technological Literacy

Students develop a conceptual understanding of artificial intelligence, digital transformation, data-driven decision making, and enterprise AI without requiring advanced programming knowledge.

---

## 4. Executive Communication

Students learn to communicate strategic recommendations through executive memoranda, consulting reports, board presentations, and professional discussions.

---

## 5. Responsible Leadership

Students examine ethical, legal, organizational, and societal implications of AI while developing strategies that create sustainable value for organizations and stakeholders.

---

# Course Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course students will be able to:

## Objective 1

Evaluate industries, competitors, and organizational environments using established strategic management frameworks while considering the impact of artificial intelligence on competitive dynamics.

---

## Objective 2

Analyze organizational resources, capabilities, and knowledge assets using the Resource-Based View, Dynamic Capabilities, and Knowledge-Based Theory of the Firm.

---

## Objective 3

Critically compare classical strategic theories with contemporary AI research and evaluate how artificial intelligence reinforces, extends, or challenges existing theoretical assumptions.

---

## Objective 4

Develop AI-enabled strategies that align organizational capabilities, competitive positioning, governance, and long-term value creation.

---

## Objective 5

Evaluate organizational readiness for artificial intelligence by assessing leadership, culture, technology, data, governance, and strategic capabilities.

---

## Objective 6

Communicate strategic recommendations appropriate for executive audiences through written analyses, consulting reports, and professional presentations.

---

## Objective 7

Apply strategic management theory to complex organizational challenges through case analyses, AI laboratories, and a semester-long consulting engagement.

---

## Objective 8

Demonstrate responsible professional judgment regarding the ethical, legal, and governance implications of enterprise artificial intelligence.

# Assurance of Learning

Student learning will be evaluated through multiple authentic assessments designed to measure executive-level strategic thinking rather than factual recall.

## Direct Assessment Methods

* Industry Analysis
* Strategic Reflection Papers
* Harvard Business School Case Analyses
* AI Laboratory Portfolio
* Executive Memoranda
* AI Strategy Consulting Project
* Executive Board Presentation
* Final Consulting Report

Each assessment measures multiple learning outcomes while progressively increasing analytical complexity throughout the semester.

---

# Learning Progression

The curriculum follows a developmental model.

| Phase                         | Weeks | Educational Focus                                         |
| ----------------------------- | ----- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Foundations                   | 1–2   | Strategic thinking, AI fundamentals, competitive analysis |
| Theoretical Development       | 3–6   | Reinterpreting classical strategy through AI              |
| Organizational Transformation | 7–8   | Governance, leadership, implementation                    |
| Strategic Integration         | 9–10  | Consulting engagement, synthesis, executive communication |

Students begin by understanding individual theories before integrating them into increasingly complex organizational analyses.

---

# Educational Philosophy

Learning occurs through repeated cycles of:

1. Theory
2. Critique
3. Comparison
4. Application
5. Reflection
6. Synthesis

Rather than mastering isolated frameworks, students progressively develop an integrated understanding of strategy suitable for organizations operating in environments increasingly shaped by artificial intelligence.

---

# Preparing Strategic Leaders

The ultimate purpose of this course extends beyond understanding strategic management.

The objective is to prepare leaders capable of guiding organizations through technological disruption while balancing innovation, organizational performance, stakeholder interests, governance, and ethical responsibility.

Graduates of this course should be prepared to contribute as consultants, analysts, managers, executives, entrepreneurs, researchers, and organizational leaders capable of integrating classical strategic thinking with emerging AI-enabled business models.

Throughout the semester students are encouraged not only to learn existing theories but also to question them, refine them, and ultimately contribute to the continuing evolution of strategic management as both an academic discipline and a professional practice.
