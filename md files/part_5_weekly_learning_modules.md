# Part V

# Course Roadmap and Weekly Learning Modules

> *"Each week of this course builds upon the last. Students progress from understanding classical strategy to extending strategic theory for an AI-enabled world."*

---

# Overview

The course is organized into ten integrated learning modules. Each module combines foundational strategic management theory with contemporary scholarship on artificial intelligence, organizational transformation, and executive leadership.

Rather than treating each week as an independent topic, the curriculum follows the AI Strategy Evolution Framework (AISE). Students repeatedly apply the framework through scholarly readings, case analyses, AI laboratories, and a semester-long consulting engagement.

Every module follows a consistent structure:

1. Strategic Question
2. Why This Matters
3. Learning Objectives
4. Classical Strategic Theory
5. Contemporary AI Scholarship
6. Harvard Business School Case (every other week)
7. AI Laboratory
8. Theory Evolution Exercise
9. Consulting Project Milestone
10. Executive Reflection

This consistent design allows students to deepen their strategic reasoning while integrating new concepts into a coherent framework.

# Developmental Learning Progression

The curriculum is intentionally sequenced to support progressively higher levels of strategic reasoning.

## Weeks 1–2: Strategic Foundations

Students establish a common understanding of strategic management, competitive advantage, and the implications of artificial intelligence for industry analysis and organizational positioning.

Primary emphasis:

* Strategic diagnosis
* Industry analysis
* Competitive positioning
* AI fundamentals

---

## Weeks 3–5: Organizational Capabilities

Students examine how organizations develop, deploy, and renew strategic capabilities while evaluating the influence of AI on organizational learning, knowledge management, and competitive advantage.

Primary emphasis:

* Dynamic capabilities
* Organizational learning
* Knowledge management
* AI-enabled capabilities

---

## Weeks 6–8: Strategic Transformation

Students transition from analysis to strategy formulation by evaluating AI-enabled business models, governance structures, organizational transformation, and enterprise implementation.

Primary emphasis:

* Strategic choice
* Core competencies
* Platform ecosystems
* Governance
* Organizational transformation

---

## Weeks 9–10: Strategic Integration

Students synthesize the semester's learning by integrating classical strategic management with contemporary AI research into a coherent executive strategy.

Primary emphasis:

* Strategic leadership
* Organizational renewal
* Theory development
* Executive communication

# Recurring Learning Activities

Each week includes the following components.

## Scholarly Dialogue

Students compare one foundational strategy paper with one contemporary AI article and evaluate how AI reshapes the assumptions of classical strategic management.

---

## Theory Evolution Exercise

Students complete a written reflection entitled:

> **If I Were Rewriting This Theory in 2026...**

The exercise requires students to:

1. Explain the original theory.
2. Identify its assumptions.
3. Evaluate how AI changes those assumptions.
4. Propose theoretical extensions.
5. Discuss implications for managers.

Each reflection contributes to the final synthesis paper.

---

## AI Laboratory

Students apply generative AI and other approved tools to analyze organizations, compare strategic alternatives, critique AI-generated recommendations, and support evidence-based decision making.

The objective is not simply to use AI, but to evaluate when AI strengthens—or weakens—strategic reasoning.

---

## Harvard Business School Case Discussion

Every other week, students analyze an organization confronting significant strategic challenges associated with artificial intelligence, digital transformation, platform competition, or organizational change.

Discussions emphasize executive decision making rather than retrospective case analysis.

---

## Consulting Project Milestone

Each week's consulting milestone builds toward the final AI Strategy Consulting Report.

Students progressively develop:

* Industry analysis
* Organizational diagnosis
* Capability assessment
* AI opportunity analysis
* Strategic recommendations
* Governance framework
* Implementation roadmap
* Executive presentation

By Week 10, these components are integrated into a board-ready strategic recommendation.

---

# The Thread That Connects Every Week

Although each module examines a different strategic theory, every week returns to the same overarching question:

> **How should strategic management theory evolve in the age of artificial intelligence?**

Students revisit this question through readings, cases, laboratories, consulting work, and reflective writing. The repetition is intentional. Strategic expertise develops through continuous refinement rather than isolated exposure to concepts.

# Preparing for Part VI

Part V provides the roadmap for the semester. Part VI builds on this roadmap by specifying the **AI Laboratory sequence**, where students learn to use artificial intelligence as a disciplined strategic thinking partner rather than simply a productivity tool.

The laboratories are designed to complement—not replace—the scholarly foundations of the course, ensuring that students graduate with both conceptual understanding and practical experience applying AI to complex strategic problems.
