# Part VI

# AI Strategy Laboratories

> *"Artificial intelligence should augment strategic thinking—not replace it."*

---

# Introduction

Artificial intelligence is rapidly becoming an integral part of executive decision making. Organizations increasingly rely on AI to analyze markets, evaluate competitors, generate strategic alternatives, forecast demand, summarize research, and support organizational transformation. However, effective strategic leadership requires more than using AI tools—it requires understanding when to trust AI, when to challenge it, and how to integrate AI-generated insights with managerial judgment.

The AI Strategy Laboratories are designed to bridge this gap.

Each laboratory places students in the role of a strategy consultant working with an executive team. Students use approved AI tools to analyze organizational problems, evaluate strategic alternatives, critique AI-generated recommendations, and develop evidence-based executive deliverables.

Every laboratory aligns with one stage of the AI Strategy Evolution Framework (AISE) and contributes directly to the semester-long AI Strategy Consulting Project.

---

# Learning Philosophy

The laboratories are guided by five principles:

1. **AI augments, but does not replace, strategic thinking.**
2. **Every AI-generated output must be critically evaluated.**
3. **Scholarly evidence takes precedence over unsupported AI claims.**
4. **Strategic recommendations require managerial judgment.**
5. **Responsible AI use includes transparency, verification, and ethical reflection.**

Students are evaluated on the quality of their reasoning rather than the sophistication of their prompts.

# Standard Laboratory Structure

Each laboratory follows a consistent sequence.

## Step 1 – Organizational Context

Students receive background information on an organization or use their semester consulting client.

The strategic challenge is introduced.

---

## Step 2 – AI-Assisted Analysis

Students use approved AI systems to:

* summarize information,
* identify strategic issues,
* generate alternatives,
* compare frameworks,
* critique recommendations,
* identify missing information,
* develop executive insights.

Students must document all AI interactions.

---

## Step 3 – Verification

Students verify AI-generated outputs using:

* peer-reviewed research,
* company reports,
* industry data,
* financial statements,
* reliable secondary sources.

Unsupported claims must be corrected or removed.

---

## Step 4 – Strategic Evaluation

Students evaluate competing strategic alternatives using:

* Porter
* Barney
* Teece
* Grant
* March
* Prahalad & Hamel
* Williamson
* AISE Framework

Recommendations must be supported by both theory and evidence.

---

## Step 5 – Executive Deliverable

Each laboratory concludes with a professional consulting deliverable prepared for senior executives.

Deliverables emphasize clarity, strategic insight, and actionable recommendations.

# Theory Evolution Exercise

Every laboratory concludes with a reflective exercise entitled:

## Rewriting Strategy for the AI Era

Students respond to the following prompt:

> **If I were rewriting this week's classical theory in 2026, I would...**

The reflection should:

1. Summarize the classical theory.
2. Identify its key assumptions.
3. Explain how AI reinforces or challenges those assumptions.
4. Propose theoretical extensions.
5. Discuss implications for managers and researchers.

Over the semester, these reflections become the foundation for the final synthesis paper, **Toward a Theory of Strategic Management in the Age of Artificial Intelligence**.

---

# AI Usage Policy for Laboratories

Students may use approved AI systems to support analysis, brainstorming, drafting, and comparison of strategic alternatives.

However:

* AI-generated content must be disclosed.
* AI outputs must be independently verified.
* Students remain responsible for all submitted work.
* Unsupported AI claims may not be cited as evidence.
* Executive recommendations must reflect independent professional judgment.

The objective of the laboratories is not prompt engineering, but the development of disciplined strategic reasoning supported by responsible AI use.

---

# Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of the AI Strategy Laboratories, students will be able to:

* Apply classical strategic frameworks to contemporary organizational challenges.
* Integrate AI into executive decision-making responsibly.
* Critically evaluate AI-generated strategic recommendations.
* Communicate evidence-based recommendations to executive audiences.
* Design AI-enabled organizational strategies grounded in theory and practice.
* Demonstrate professional judgment in environments characterized by technological uncertainty.

The laboratories prepare students to function as strategic analysts, consultants, and organizational leaders capable of leveraging artificial intelligence while maintaining the critical thinking, ethical reasoning, and strategic perspective essential to effective executive leadership.
